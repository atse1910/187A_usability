05. Error Prevention

Error Recognition and Recovery

On University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website, Help and Documentation is not as necessary. A site index is provided; however, its naming convention differs from the standardized nomenclature of a site map. Instead of calling a "Site Map", University of Louisiana at Lafayette calls its site map "Index A-Z". This can be quite inefficient if it deviates from the norm.
— Jacob Nielsen

Error prevention is a critical aspect of website design. Frequent errors can cause website vulnerabilities including DNS attacks and viral computer threats. One way the University of Louisiana at Lafayette prevents users from experiencing errors is through the usage of asterisks and required fields. The asterisks informs users that the information is required and cannot continue until the required information is filled. In order to prevent errors on forms, the University of Louisiana at Lafayette implements Javascript codes to automatically checks if users omitted a certain piece of information in the required fields in real time while users are entering these pieces of information. If information is missing, an exclamation mark appears to quickly alert the users that information is missing.
Unfortunately, University of Louisiana at Lafayette's error prevention is not entirely full-proof of errors. Users can enter any kinds of data rather than specific data in specific form fields. Users can enter alphabetical texts in number fields which can be error-prone.
