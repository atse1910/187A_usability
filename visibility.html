01. Visibility

Visibility of system status.

The system should always keep users informed about what is going on,through appropriate feedback within reasonable time.."
— Jacob Nielsen

    The University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website provides an exceptional piece of visibility for users to navigate around. However, there are a few errors with categories overly-too broad that do not encompass the entire scope of information that needs to be displayed. On the website, I have noted some advantages and disadvantages of the main home page.
  1. Users are greeted with the current page they are at. It is useful to let users what they are looking at while browsing. Unfortunately, the navigation bar is not highlighted to display the current page that the user is currently viewing. By highlighting the categories within the navigation bar, users can learn and know the information they seek quicker.
  2. University of Louisiana at Lafayette provides a navigation bar at the top of each page on its website. The advantage of this can be two-sided. For one thing, the navigation is organized. Unfortunately, not every piece of information is categorized in the navigation bar in a visible manner.
Side bars are one of the most powerful options that make using websites more efficiently. However, we found that the side menu of the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website as a big problem. Firstly, UL's website needs to rethink to think about the main function of a side bar on websites. When users browse through websites, the side bar is one of the crucial navigation tools use when the top navigation bar fails.
Unfortunately, not every page on University of Louisiana at Lafayette's provides the consistency and visibility for information. As shown in this image, the visibility of information does not have a direct correlation between the news and communication page. It is in fact the same page. This inconsistency can generate initial confusions for users as it may put users in a situation where it is difficult to know where users are.
