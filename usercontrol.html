03. User Control

User control and freedom.

Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo.
— Jacob Nielsen

The University of Louisiana at Lafayette lacks complete user control when going around the website. For instance, when users click on "Campus Map", a PDF is shown rather than an interactive file. Without this sort interactivity to zoom and pan a map, users are not entirely in control.
Navigation is not always precise. When users are browsing at the University's Library's website, links and buttons are often misplaced and misdirected. For instance, when users click either on 'Library' or 'Research', users are directed to a different kind of page than expected. Unfortunately, users also lack the ability to return the previous page.
Often times in web designing today, galleries are incorporated with navigation tools, specifically navigation arrows, to move back and forth between photos. Unfortunately, University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website is missing this feature - which is a crucial navigation element when it comes to photo navigation. Ultimately, navigation deficiency on the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's is at a high moderate high.
