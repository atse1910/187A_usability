04. Consistency

Consistency and Standards

Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions...
— Jacob Nielsen

The University of Louisiana at Lafayette displays a moderate level of website design inconsistency. The usage of color in graphics and text are somewhat consistent; however, naming conventions consist with links is not always the case. For instance, when users click on "Student Life", they are directed to a page with a header called "Current Students." Users would expect that when clicking on "Student Life", users will be greeted with Campus Facts. Unfortunately, it does not say that. Rather, it directs users to "Current Students" - a category that is misplaced, mislinked and irrelevant.
The University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website overall navigation system could be generally confusing for many users. Sidebar navigation and top navigation bars are are all poorly organized with different tabs for a single category under different titles. Also, the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website also displays a high degree of redundancy - repeating things over when it is not necessary. Also, broken links are present that users are sometimes redirected to a 404 Page Error page. Furthermore, consistency diminishes with also page headers. As stated before, certain pages do not page the original desired title. For example, if users click on "Student Life", users are redirected to a page containing a top header banner saying "Current Students." With high redundancy and inconsistency, the overall information architecture can be highly compromised.
