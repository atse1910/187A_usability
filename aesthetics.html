08. Aesthetics

Aesthetics and Design

Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility.
— Jacob Nielsen
The general aesthetics of University of Louisiana at Lafayette is subjectively dry and boring. The usage of red and white clearly reflects the school's colors; however, general placement of its information and the general architecture debilitates the website from its potential to be more exciting. Furthermore, physical appeal of the website only lasts less than a few seconds - indicating that users are not entirely drawn by the configuration of the website.

As users browse further in-depth within the site, users begin to see that the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's logo is always in the background. Along with the greenish-yellow-like background tint, aesthetically, the colors used by University of Louisiana at Lafayette do not follow the Color Wheel Theory. In art, the Color Wheel Theory states contrasting colors on the color wheel are complements of one another that makes appearances stand out. However, University of Louisiana at Lafayette's website does not do so. Along with red on its navigation bar and text, it is apparent that UL's appeal deteriorates over time.

On University of Louisiana at Lafayette's departmental websites, the same theme of red on greenish-yellow is present. Likewise, on University of Louisiana at Lafayette's library website, HTML errors with white space misuses threatens the attractiveness of the website. Overall, the Library's webpage threatens a user's ability to find information more quickly as both graphics and information consumes unnecessary of space leaving white space.

Fortunately, not all deparmental websites at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette incorporates the same color scheme as the main Home Page to the University. Departments like the Visual Arts Department and Biology Department deviates from the color scheme of the University and overall theme.
